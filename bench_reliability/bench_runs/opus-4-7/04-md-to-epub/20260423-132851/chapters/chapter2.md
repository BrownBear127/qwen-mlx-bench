# Chapter 2: The Market Square

By mid-morning the square was alive with activity. Farmers displayed baskets of
apples, pears, and late-season plums. A blacksmith demonstrated his craft near
the fountain, sparks dancing in the sunlight.

Maria found her friend Thomas at the woodcarver's stall, examining a small
figurine of a fox. "Remarkable detail," he said, turning it in his hands. Maria
nodded — the craftsman had captured something almost lifelike in the tiny wooden
animal.

They wandered from stall to stall, sampling honey cakes and roasted chestnuts.
The festival always brought out the best in the village: neighbors who rarely
spoke found common ground over shared food and music. Even the mayor, usually
stern-faced, was spotted laughing with the children near the puppet show.
