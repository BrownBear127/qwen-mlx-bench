# Chapter 3: The Evening Dance

As twilight settled over the village, lanterns were lit around the square. A
small orchestra — two fiddles, an accordion, and a double bass — began to play
a lively waltz. Couples moved onto the cobblestones, their laughter mingling
with the music.

Maria stood at the edge of the crowd, watching. The festival had exceeded her
expectations this year. The harvest had been generous, the weather kind, and
the villagers seemed lighter than they had in months. She felt a deep gratitude
for this place and these people.

Thomas appeared beside her with two cups of warm cider. "To the harvest," he
said, raising his cup. Maria smiled and raised hers in return. Above them the
first stars appeared, and the music played on into the night.
